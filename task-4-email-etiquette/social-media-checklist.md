## Do's - Responsible Social Media Use

| # | Do |
|---|-----|
| 1 | **Keep your profile professional** - Use a clear, appropriate profile picture and bio, especially on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub that employers and faculty may view. |
| 2 | **Think before you post** - Always ask yourself if your post could be misunderstood, offensive, or harmful before sharing it publicly. |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings** - Regularly review and update your privacy settings on all platforms to control who can see your content and personal information. |
| 4 | **Give credit when sharing** - Always credit the original creator when sharing someone else's work, images, or ideas to avoid plagiarism and copyright issues. |
| 5 | **Report harmful content** - If you come across bullying, misinformation, or inappropriate content, use the platform's report feature to flag it responsibly. |

---

## Don'ts - What to Avoid Online

| # | Don't |
|---|--------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal information publicly** - Avoid posting your phone number, home address, Aadhaar details, or bank information on any social media platform. |
| 2 | **Don't engage with or spread misinformation** - Always verify news and information from trusted sources before sharing, especially during sensitive events. |
| 3 | **Don't post in the heat of the moment** - Avoid posting angry, emotional, or impulsive content. What goes online can be difficult or impossible to fully delete. |
| 4 | **Don't accept requests from strangers blindly** - Avoid connecting with unknown individuals who have no mutual connections or a suspicious or incomplete profile. |
| 5 | **Don't share confidential matters publicly** - Never share exam questions, internal college communications, or sensitive academic information on public platforms. |

---

## Reflection

Poor digital communication on social media can have serious real-world consequences.
A common example is students posting complaints or negative opinions about their
college, professors, or classmates publicly, which has led to disciplinary action in
several institutions. Even posts meant to be private can be screenshotted and shared.

What could have been done differently: The student could have raised concerns through
proper internal channels such as emailing the faculty or speaking to a counsellor,
instead of venting publicly online. Responsible digital communication means choosing
the right platform and tone for every message.
