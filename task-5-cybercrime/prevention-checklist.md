## Stay Safe Online - Prevention Checklist for College Students

| # | Prevention Tip |
|---|----------------|
| 1 | **Never click on links in emails or SMS messages** - Always type the official website address directly into your browser instead of clicking links, even if the message appears to come from a trusted source like your bank or college. |
| 2 | **Use strong and unique passwords** - Create passwords that are at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple accounts. |
| 3 | **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** - Turn on 2FA on all important accounts including email, college portals, and social media. This adds an extra layer of security even if your password is compromised. |
| 4 | **Verify before you trust** - If you receive an urgent message claiming to be from your bank, college, or any authority, call the official helpline directly to verify before taking any action. |
| 5 | **Keep your software and apps updated** - Always install the latest updates for your operating system, browser, and apps. Updates often contain critical security patches that protect against known vulnerabilities. |
| 6 | **Use only trusted and secure Wi-Fi networks** - Avoid accessing your bank accounts, college portals, or any sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi. If necessary, use a VPN for an added layer of protection. |
| 7 | **Check website URLs carefully** - Before entering any login credentials or personal information, verify that the website URL is correct and starts with https. Watch for small spelling changes such as "vvitbhopal.ac.in" instead of "vitbhopal.ac.in". |
| 8 | **Do not share OTPs with anyone** - No legitimate bank, company, or government agency will ever ask for your OTP over a call or message. Sharing an OTP is one of the most common ways financial fraud occurs. |

## UPI and Financial Safety Tips

| # | UPI and Financial Safety Tip |
|---|-------------------------------|
| 1 | **Never share your UPI PIN with anyone** - Your UPI PIN is like your ATM PIN. No bank representative, customer care agent, or government official will ever ask for it. If someone does, it is a scam. |
| 2 | **Be cautious of "collect requests" on UPI apps** - Fraudsters often send fake collect requests on apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm, pretending to be from a legitimate source. You do not need to enter your PIN to receive money - if an app asks for your PIN to receive a payment, decline immediately. |

## Reporting Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know has been a victim of cybercrime, report it immediately through the following official channels:

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 (available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week)
- **Local Police Station:** File an FIR if financial loss has occurred

Reporting quickly increases the chances of recovering lost funds and preventing
the attacker from targeting others.
