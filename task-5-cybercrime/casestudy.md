## Case Study: The Fake Bank Alert

### What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which an attacker impersonates a trusted
organisation or individual, such as a bank, government agency, or popular online
platform, through a deceptive email, SMS, or fake website. The goal is to trick
the victim into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, credit
card numbers, or bank account details.

---

### How It Typically Happens - Step by Step

1. The attacker sends a convincing email or SMS that appears to come from a
   legitimate source, such as the victim's bank or a platform like Google, Amazon,
   or a government portal.

2. The message creates a sense of urgency, for example: "Your account has been
   suspended. Click the link below to verify your identity immediately."

3. The victim clicks the link, which redirects them to a fake website that looks
   identical to the original, complete with logos, fonts, and layout.

4. The victim enters their login credentials, OTP, or card details on the fake page,
   believing it to be genuine.

5. The attacker captures this information in real time and uses it to access the
   victim's real accounts, make unauthorized transactions, or sell the data.

6. The victim often remains unaware until they notice unauthorized activity in their
   account or receive a bank alert.

---

### Who is Usually Targeted?

Phishing attacks target a wide range of individuals, but the most common victims
include:

- College students who use multiple online platforms and may not recognize warning signs
- Elderly individuals who are less familiar with digital fraud techniques
- Working professionals who receive a high volume of emails daily and may click
  links hastily
- People who have recently made online transactions and may expect follow-up
  communications from banks or e-commerce platforms

---

### Consequences

The consequences of a successful phishing attack can be severe:

- **Financial loss** - Unauthorized transactions can drain bank accounts or max out
  credit cards before the victim is even aware.
- **Identity theft** - Stolen personal information can be used to open fake accounts,
  apply for loans, or commit fraud in the victim's name.
- **Academic account compromise** - For students, a phished university login can
  expose grades, personal records, and internal communications.
- **Emotional distress** - Victims often experience anxiety, embarrassment, and a
  loss of trust in digital platforms.
- **Legal complications** - In rare cases, attackers misuse the victim's identity for
  illegal activities, putting the victim in a difficult legal position.

---

### Real-World Reference

According to the Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In), phishing
attacks targeting Indian bank customers and UPI users have increased significantly
in recent years. Many attacks are carried out through fake SMS messages impersonating
the State Bank of India, HDFC Bank, and government portals such as the income tax
e-filing website.

Source: CERT-In Annual Report | cybercrime.gov.in

## Reflection

The most surprising thing discovered while researching this topic was that many
modern phishing websites now use HTTPS, meaning they display the padlock icon
that users are typically told to trust. This shows that simply checking for a
secure connection is no longer enough to verify a website's authenticity.

One habit that will be personally changed as a result of this task is to never
click on links received through email or SMS. Instead, the correct web address
will always be typed directly into the browser. Two-factor authentication will
also be enabled on all important accounts as an added layer of protection.
